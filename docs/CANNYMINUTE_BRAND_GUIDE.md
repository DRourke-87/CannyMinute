# CannyMinute Brand Guide

## Brand Essence

### Purpose
Help people reduce impulse buying by inserting a calm, practical pause at the moment of purchase.

### Brand promise
A minute of clarity before you commit.

### Personality
Calm, straight talking, friendly, lightly Northern, never preachy.

### Positioning
Checkout pause and reflection across shopping apps and browsers on Android, privacy first.

## Audience

### Primary
Mobile shoppers who buy impulsively, especially in the evening, and people who want simple guardrails without judgement.

### Secondary
Budgeters, debt payoff communities, minimalists, sustainability-minded shoppers, and ADHD or neurodivergent users who benefit from friction and structure.

## Voice And Tone

### Voice principles
- Warm and supportive, not moralising.
- Practical, not fluffy.
- Short sentences and clear actions.
- Light humour is fine, sarcasm is not.

### Words to use
Pause, minute, steady, quick check, save for later, cool down, guardrail, intention, choice.

### Words to avoid
Shame, guilt, failure, bad habits, addiction language, "you should".

### Example microcopy
- "Quick check, then you can still buy it."
- "No judgement, just a minute."
- "Save it for later and decide with a clear head."

## Naming And Usage

### Brand name
CannyMinute

### Spoken
"Canny minute"

### Tagline options
- A minute before you buy
- Pause before you pay
- Wait now, decide better later

## Visual Identity

### Logo style
Flat, modern, Material-aligned. Simple shapes, consistent stroke weight, strong legibility at small sizes.

### Icon concept
Hourglass in a rounded square with a pause symbol in the neck, using a blue and grey palette. Keep details minimal so it reads at 24px.

### Do
- Use generous whitespace.
- Use clear iconography for time and pause.
- Keep corner radii consistent across icon and UI surfaces.

### Do not
- Use harsh warning red as a primary color.
- Use overly detailed illustrations.
- Use cluttered gradients or glossy effects in product UI.

## Color Palette

### Primary
- Deep blue for trust and focus.
- Cool grey for structure.
- White or near-white backgrounds for clarity.

### Secondary
- Soft blue for highlights.
- Muted slate for text and dividers.

### Accent
- One restrained bright blue accent for key actions, used sparingly.

### Accessibility notes
- Ensure sufficient contrast for body text and buttons.
- Never rely on color alone to convey state; pair with labels or icons.

## Typography

### Approach
Use Material-aligned sans serif typography (Compose Material defaults are acceptable).

### Hierarchy
- Headings: clear, not shouty.
- Body: readable with comfortable line spacing.
- Buttons: short verbs with consistent case.

## Iconography And Components

### Icon style
Use Material iconography consistently (filled or outlined, not mixed arbitrarily).

### Corner radius
Use one rounded corner language across cards, buttons, sheets, and app icon.

### Motion
Subtle and short fades/slides. Avoid warning-like or jarring motion.

## Intervention Experience

### Core prompt
A calm interstitial that feels like a friend tapping your shoulder, not a hard block.

### The three questions
1. Do I need it?
2. Can I afford it?
3. Have I checked whether it is available cheaper elsewhere?

### Recommended layout
- Title: "CannyMinute" or "Quick check".
- Reassurance line: "Take a minute, you can still buy it after."
- Three questions with quick tap answers.
- A visible countdown timer.
- Actions: "Continue" (after timer), "Save for later", "Close".

### Defaults
- Timer starts at 10 seconds and is user-adjustable.
- "Save for later" should be prominent.
- "Allow once" appears only after question completion.

## Trust And Privacy Messaging

### One sentence promise
On-device only, no data sold, no screen text stored unless diagnostics is explicitly enabled.

### Permission explanation
"CannyMinute uses Accessibility so it can detect when a checkout screen appears and show your pause screen. Your screen content stays on your phone."

### FAQ topics
- Why Accessibility is needed.
- What data is processed and stored.
- How to disable or pause the app.
- How to allow-list certain apps.
- How false positives are handled.

## Marketing Visuals And Content

### Screenshot style
Large headline per screen, one idea per image.

### Content themes
- "I was about to buy this, then I paused."
- Savings wins without shaming.
- Practical tips: price checks and budget framing.
- Permission trust explainers.
